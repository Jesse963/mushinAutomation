RPA Implementation Lifecycle

  • Assessment

    • In the assessment stage, your business evaluates its processes to identify suitable candidates for automation. You assess the feasibility and potential benefits of implementing RPA, considering factors such as task complexity, volume, and standardization. Design and

  • Development

    • Once the processes are selected for automation, Mushin developers collaborate with your business's subject matter experts to understand the workflows and design the automation solution. Our skilled team creates and configures bots that mimic human actions, interacting with your applications and systems.

  • Testing and Validation

    • The designed bots undergo rigorous testing and validation by Mushin staff to ensure their accuracy and functionality. Test scenarios are executed, and any issues or errors are identified and resolved. User acceptance testing may also involve your key stakeholders to ensure their satisfaction and confidence in the automation solution.

  • Deployment

    • After successful testing and validation, the bots are ready for deployment within your business's environment. Mushin staff assists in the installation and integration of the bots with your existing systems and applications. The deployment process is carefully managed to ensure a smooth transition to production, minimizing any disruption to your ongoing operations.

  • Operation and Monitoring

    • Once deployed, the bots start performing their automated tasks. Mushin staff continuously monitor their performance, reliability, and adherence to your defined business rules. We collect logs and metrics to track their activities and identify areas for optimization.

  • Maintenance and Optimisation

    • Your RPA bots require ongoing maintenance and optimisation. Mushin provides regular updates and patches to address any issues, enhance security, or introduce new features. Our team ensures that the bots operate efficiently, accurately, and respond promptly to your business needs.

  • Retirement

    • As your business processes evolve or become obsolete, there may come a time when certain bots are no longer needed. The retirement stage involves decommissioning and retiring the bots that have reached the end of their lifecycle. Mushin assists in proper documentation, knowledge transfer, and identifies potential replacement opportunities to support your evolving automation needs.
